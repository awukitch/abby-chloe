What is cancer?

Cancer is a disease that occurs when mutated cells grow uncontrollably resulting in the invasion of healthy cells.

Why is childhood cancer different?

Childhood cancer can differ from adult cancer in terms of cause, treatment, and effect on the individual's lifestyle. In children, cancer often develops as the result of genetic mutations which occur before the child's birth. On the other hand, adult cancer is often easier detected due to a range of factors, including life choices like smoking. Radiation treatment such as chemotherapy also affects children differently than adults. Although children's bodies often respond to chemo quicker, it can cause a wide range of long term side effects (infertility, sstunted growth, etc.) which affect the child for the rest of his/her life. This can cause mental disorders including depression and anxiety, therfore it is important that the child has access to both physical and mental help alike.
